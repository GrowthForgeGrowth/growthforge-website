AI Marketing Glossary

Clear definitions for growth leaders building AI-powered demand systems.

Core Terms

AI SEO

AI SEO is the practice of improving search visibility using both traditional optimization and AI-assisted analysis, prioritization, and content workflows. It encompasses technical SEO, keyword strategy, content systems, and Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) — integrated to capture demand from both traditional search engines and AI-generated answers. See how GrowthForge applies AI SEO for B2B SaaS →

GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)

GEO is the discipline of optimizing content and entity signals so that AI-powered search engines — ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Gemini, and Perplexity — cite your brand in their generated answers. GEO differs from traditional SEO: it targets AI retrieval and citation rather than keyword rankings and click-through rates. As search shifts to AI-first interfaces, GEO has become a critical channel for demand capture. Explore GrowthForge's GEO services →

Entity Optimization

Entity optimization improves clarity around who you are, what you do, and how concepts on your site relate to each other semantically. Strong entity signals — consistent Organization schema, author markup, and concept relationships — support both traditional SEO and GEO performance. Weak entity signals are one of the most common reasons a brand is excluded from AI-generated answers.

Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO)

CRO is the systematic improvement of landing pages, user journeys, and conversion architecture to increase the percentage of visitors who take meaningful actions — demo bookings, form submissions, or purchases. CRO must precede paid media scaling: improving conversion before increasing ad spend multiplies ROI. Explore GrowthForge's CRO partnership options →

Attribution Confidence

Attribution confidence is the degree to which decision-makers trust performance data enough to invest and scale with conviction. Without accurate attribution across channels, budgets flow to the wrong acquisition sources and true CAC is systematically underestimated or overestimated.

Growth System

A growth system is an integrated operating model that aligns strategy, acquisition, conversion, measurement, and iteration toward business outcomes. It differs from a collection of campaigns in that its components are interdependent: SEO, paid media, CRO, automation, and analytics reinforce each other. Learn how GrowthForge builds growth systems →

Growth Consultancy

A growth consultancy is a strategic business partner that takes accountability for growth outcomes — revenue, qualified leads, and CAC — rather than just service deliverables. It differs from a traditional marketing agency in that it aligns all channels and activities to a shared business goal, provides executive-level strategic guidance, and measures success by business metrics rather than service outputs. Compare growth consultancy vs marketing agency →

BOFU / MOFU / TOFU (Funnel Stages)

BOFU (Bottom of Funnel), MOFU (Middle of Funnel), and TOFU (Top of Funnel) describe where a buyer is in their decision journey. BOFU content targets buyers ready to purchase (e.g., pricing pages, comparison pages, demo CTAs). MOFU content helps buyers evaluate options (e.g., use-case pages, comparison guides). TOFU content builds awareness (e.g., educational blog posts, glossaries). Effective SEO requires content at all three stages, with commercial priority given to BOFU and MOFU pages.

Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

CAC is the total cost to acquire a single new customer, including marketing and sales spend. Reducing CAC while maintaining or growing lead volume is a primary goal of growth system optimization. AI SEO and CRO are the highest-leverage levers for compounding CAC reduction over time.

Why This Glossary Exists

AI-era growth language evolves quickly. This glossary gives operators and executives a shared vocabulary for better strategic decisions. Terms are kept concise and accurate — no filler or keyword inflation.
