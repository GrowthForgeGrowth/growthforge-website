AI Search Authority Checklist

A practical framework for deciding whether your site is ready to earn trust, citations, and commercial visibility in AI search.

Prepared by Charlie Chan, Founder & Growth Strategist at GrowthForge. Use this checklist before scaling AI SEO, GEO, or conversion-focused content production.

How to use this page

  • Score each checkpoint from 0-2: missing, partially visible, or clearly published.
  • Prioritize visible trust assets before adding more top-of-funnel content.
  • Use the links in each section to review how GrowthForge structures commercial and trust pages.

The 5-Part Scoring Framework

Strong AI search authority is usually the result of five visible signal groups working together. Score each group from 0-10 for a fast diagnostic.

1. Entity Clarity

Can a buyer or AI system quickly confirm who you are, what you do, and who leads the work?

2. Trust Pages

Are your about, contact, editorial, and policy pages complete enough to validate your business context?

3. Commercial Proof

Do service pages show methodology, outcomes framing, attribution, and the next step for serious buyers?

4. Citation Structure

Are definitions, processes, criteria, and takeaways written in a scannable format AI engines can extract?

5. Authority Flow

Do your glossary, trust pages, and resource pages pass relevance and internal link equity to priority money pages?

Checklist: What to Publish Before You Push Harder on AI Search

Each category below includes visible checkpoints that improve both buyer confidence and AI citation likelihood.

1) Entity clarity & authorship

  • Founder or lead expert is named on key commercial pages.
  • About page explains experience, operating principles, and how success is measured.
  • Contact details, service descriptions, and positioning are consistent across the site.
  • Buyers can move from informational pages to a clear commercial next step without guessing.

2) Trust pages that reduce verification friction

  • About page explains who the business serves, how it works, and how engagements are scoped.
  • Editorial standards explain authorship, review, fact-checking, and correction practices.
  • Contact page explains best contact methods, response expectations, and inquiry paths.
  • Pricing or service pages make the commercial model legible without requiring a sales call.

Review GrowthForge editorial standards →  |  See contact paths and response expectations →

3) Commercial proof buyers can evaluate quickly

  • Service pages explain the operating method, not just the offer headline.
  • Outcome framing is tied to business metrics such as qualified pipeline, CAC, or conversion quality.
  • Decision-stage internal links connect use-case, comparison, pricing, and contact pages.
  • Claims remain factual and visible; no hidden proof or inflated statistics.

4) Citation-ready page structure

  • Each priority page has one clear H1, logical H2/H3 hierarchy, and answer-first paragraphs.
  • Definitions, processes, criteria, and key takeaways are easy to extract as standalone passages.
  • Metadata and schema reflect visible content exactly.
  • Important pages include concise summaries a buyer or AI system can quote accurately.

See a GEO page with definitions, process, and criteria →

5) Internal authority flow

  • Glossary, insights, trust pages, and reference assets link naturally to service and pricing pages.
  • Commercial pages link back to reference content that supports evaluation and due diligence.
  • Anchor text is descriptive and specific, not generic or repetitive.
  • Priority money pages receive repeated, relevant links from multiple page types.

Quick Takeaways

  • Authority in AI search is easier to earn when trust pages and commercial pages reinforce each other.
  • The fastest credibility gains usually come from clarifying authorship, methodology, and commercial transparency.
  • Reference assets should send authority to money pages with natural, descriptive internal links.
  • If a claim is not visible, verifiable, and easy to interpret, do not mark it up or scale it.
